# Cover 3 Notes

Cover 3 keeps three defenders deep and usually asks four underneath defenders to rally downhill. It is structurally sound against vertical shots, but disciplined offenses can still attack the seams, curl windows, and flats.

Wide receivers who win on deep outs, posts, and seam benders often produce well against Cover 3 looks. Tight ends can also stress hook defenders when route spacing forces the underneath zones to choose between multiple threats.

Defenses that rotate into Cover 3 late can disguise intentions, but communication errors show up quickly when offenses use motion before the snap.
